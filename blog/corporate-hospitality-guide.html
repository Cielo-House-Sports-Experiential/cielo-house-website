Hospitality Events Guide

How to Organise Corporate Hospitality at Major Australian Events

By Britt Niven Managing Director
7 min read
2026

Corporate hospitality at a major Australian event is one of the most powerful tools in a brand's relationship-building arsenal. Done well, it creates the kind of meaningful connections with clients, partners, and prospects that no amount of email marketing or LinkedIn outreach can replicate. Done poorly, it's an expensive afternoon that nobody remembers for the wrong reasons.

At Cielo House, we've designed and delivered VIP hospitality programs at some of Australia's biggest sporting events from the Supercars Gold Coast 500 to the Australian Grand Prix and NRL State of Origin. Here's what we've learned about what separates an unforgettable program from an ordinary one.

Step 1: Define Your Objective Before Anything Else

This sounds obvious, but it's where most hospitality programs go wrong. "We want to entertain our clients" is not an objective it's a starting point. Before you book a suite or send a single invitation, get clear on:

  • Who are the priority guests? (New prospects, existing clients, key stakeholders?)
  • What relationship outcomes are you hoping to achieve?
  • What do you want guests to walk away saying about your brand?
  • How will you measure success?

The answers to these questions shape every subsequent decision from the event you choose to the catering menu to how you structure the day.

Step 2: Choose the Right Event for Your Audience

Australia has an extraordinary events calendar motorsport, AFL, NRL, horse racing, tennis, cricket, golf, surfing. The event you choose says something about your brand. Before committing, ask:

  • Does this event resonate with our target guest profile?
  • Is the venue and atmosphere conducive to the conversations we want to have?
  • Does the event timeline suit a business hospitality format (i.e., enough structured time for relationship building)?

A major motorsport event like the Supercars Gold Coast 500 or the Australian Grand Prix offers a natural conversation starter, a shared emotional experience, and a format that gives guests something to focus on between conversations which actually makes relationship-building easier, not harder.

"The right event for your guests is the one they'll be excited to tell their friends they attended."

Step 3: Select Your Hosting Format

Corporate hospitality comes in several formats, each with different pros, cons, and price points:

  • Private suite or corporate box: The premium option. Exclusive space, dedicated catering, privacy for conversations. Best for senior-level relationship building with smaller guest lists.
  • Branded marquee or pavilion: A middle ground shared atmosphere with other corporate guests, but with your branding and exclusive catering within your allocated space.
  • Premium general hospitality package: Event-provided hospitality packages in a premium precinct. Lower cost, less exclusivity, but can still deliver a quality experience with the right additions.
  • Bespoke hosted experience: A fully customised program designed around the event, with pre-event activations, on-site host management, curated catering, and post-event follow-up built in.

Step 4: Build Your Guest List Strategically

The guest list is where the real work happens. Every invitation should be considered: who genuinely benefits from attending, who will create the right energy in the room, and whose presence will lead to meaningful outcomes for your business?

A common mistake is over-inviting packing a suite with 40 guests when 20 would create a far more intimate, memorable experience. Think about guest mix carefully: too many internal team members and the event loses its client focus; too many guests at the same level without enough host oversight and the day can feel under-managed.

Step 5: Design the Guest Journey

Great corporate hospitality doesn't just happen it's choreographed. Think through every moment from a guest's perspective:

  1. Pre-event communication: Save-the-date, detailed logistics, dress code, parking/transport information send these early and follow up.
  2. Arrival experience: How are guests greeted? Is there signage? A dedicated host? Drinks waiting?
  3. During the event: Catering timing, viewing arrangements, entertainment program, downtime for conversation.
  4. The unexpected touch: A personalised gift, access to a player or personality, a surprising menu moment something that makes this event genuinely different.
  5. Departure and follow-up: A genuine thank-you, a memorable leave-behind, and a prompt follow-up that continues the relationship conversation.

Step 6: Get the Details Right

In premium hospitality, the details are the experience. The quality of the catering, the attentiveness of the host team, the comfort of the seating, the personalisation in the room these are the things guests remember and talk about. Conversely, it's the logistical failures that guests remember longest: the delayed car, the wrong dietary requirement, the confusion about parking.

Working with an experienced hospitality partner means these details are handled systematically not left to chance on the day.

Step 7: Measure and Build On It

After the event, capture feedback from your guest-facing team. Which conversations progressed? What did guests say? Were there any moments that fell short? The best corporate hospitality programs improve with each iteration because they're treated as a strategic investment, not just a party.
